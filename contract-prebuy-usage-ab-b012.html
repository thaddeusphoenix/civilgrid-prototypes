Contract Pre-Buy & Usage (A/B)
Purpose

One customer-facing page, two formatting takes, toggled from the Prototype bar. Same data both ways — which layout shows how many projects were bought and how many you've used fastest? No pacing, no projections, no expected-usage framing, and no dollar figures — project count only.

Linear project

Billing & Fee Transparency ↗

Layout A — bar
  • One usage-bar card carries used/pool/remaining (no separate stat tiles — b007 folded them in)
  • Monthly bar chart below it, spanning the full contract term (Dec '25 – Dec '26) — total projects per month, no per-tier breakdown
  • Scannable, familiar dashboard pattern
Layout B — gauge + trajectory
  • Radial gauge (ring only) with the numbers in a text block beside it, not overlaid on top (b011)
  • Cumulative projects-used-to-date line under the pool ceiling — hover the points
  • Calm, one-glance status of the contract
What to notice

Toggle A/B in the header. The pool is denominated in Project Units (project count), not raw acreage/mileage — grounded in CivilGrid's real Order Form pricing (Project Unit = 0.5 mi or 20 acres per project). Every fact has exactly one canonical spot on the page: in Layout A that's the Contract usage bar card (used/pool/remaining, no separate tiles); in Layout B that's the gauge + adjacent text block. The contract term (start/end) lives in the shared page subhead now — a two-line card just to show two dates was overkill (b010). Tier breakdowns stay out (v1 is deliberately simple) but the monthly trend is back in as a single total-only bar chart (b011). Still no utilization %, run rate, projected usage, or dollar figures. Both stamp the feedback form with which layout you were viewing.
